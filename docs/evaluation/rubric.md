# Expert Review Rubric


Source basis: the proposal identifies curriculum alignment, scientific accuracy, pedagogical appropriateness, clarity, usability, and production practicality as feasibility criteria. The platform also tracks narration-visual fit from the earlier research scope.

## Rating Scale

1 = poor
2 = weak
3 = acceptable
4 = good
5 = excellent

Each criterion includes a required rating and optional comment field. Comments should link to specific artifacts or moments where possible.

## Criteria

### Curriculum Alignment

Definition: The prototype reflects the selected Tanzanian curriculum topic, standard, learning objective, and source references.

Example prompts: Does the artifact match the cited TIE topic? Are any claims unsupported by the source?

Evidence linkage: CurriculumItem, CandidateTopic, TopicBrief, GenerationManifest.

### Scientific Accuracy

Definition: The explanation and visuals are scientifically correct for the primary-level topic.

Example prompts: Are processes, quantities, labels, and causal relationships accurate? Are there misleading simplifications?

Evidence linkage: ScriptDraft, VisualArtifact, NarrationArtifact, AssemblyArtifact.

### Pedagogical Appropriateness

Definition: The artifact is suitable for primary learners in language, pacing, complexity, examples, and cognitive load.

Example prompts: Is the explanation age-appropriate? Does it avoid unnecessary abstraction or overload?

Evidence linkage: TopicBrief, AssemblyArtifact, evaluator comments.

### Clarity and Coherence

Definition: The learner-facing sequence is understandable and logically organized.

Example prompts: Does the artifact move from setup to explanation to conclusion? Are transitions clear?

Evidence linkage: ScriptDraft, AssemblyArtifact.

### Narration-Visual Fit

Definition: Narration and visuals reinforce each other without contradiction or timing mismatch.

Example prompts: Does the narration describe what appears on screen? Are labels and visual events synchronized?

Evidence linkage: NarrationArtifact, VisualArtifact, AssemblyArtifact.

### Usability

Definition: Evaluators can access, inspect, and review the prototype without unnecessary friction.

Example prompts: Is the review interface clear? Can the evaluator identify the artifact, topic, and rubric task?

Evidence linkage: EvaluationInvitation, RubricResponse, channel logs.

### Production Practicality

Definition: The workflow can feasibly produce prototypes with reasonable time, iteration, resource, and review burden.

Example prompts: Are the artifacts good enough for expert feasibility review? Are failures or refinement needs visible and manageable?

Evidence linkage: GenerationRun, RefinementLog, ExportBatch, ReplayAttempt.

## Comment Fields

Each criterion should allow:

- criterion-specific comment;
- artifact-specific comment;
- optional severity flag;
- optional suggested revision;
- evaluator confidence.
