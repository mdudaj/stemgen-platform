# Research Scope

## Source basis

This scope is aligned with the proposal at `/home/jmduda/Desktop/UDSM/Dissertation/MSc Research Proposal - Generative AI for STEM Animation v20260412.pdf` and the earlier dissertation platform docs under `/home/jmduda/Desktop/FikraBytez/dissertation/docs`. The proposal title is "Investigating the Feasibility of a Generative AI-Based System for Producing Curriculum-Aligned STEM Animations and Narrations in Tanzanian Primary Education."

`stemgen-platform` supports a feasibility-focused design science study of whether a generative AI-based system can produce curriculum-aligned STEM animations and narrations for Tanzanian primary education.

The study is not a classroom effectiveness study. Pupils are not direct participants in this phase. Teachers and subject/curriculum experts evaluate curriculum alignment, scientific accuracy, pedagogical appropriateness, clarity, usability, narration-visual fit, and production practicality.

The platform must preserve curriculum sources, topic decisions, generation provenance, artifacts, expert reviews, exports, and replay evidence.


## Proposal-derived evaluation posture

The proposal frames feasibility around output quality and production practicality. Structured expert review should assess curriculum alignment, scientific accuracy, pedagogical appropriateness, clarity and coherence, narration-visual fit, usability, and production practicality, including time required, extent of manual intervention, and workflow difficulties.

The planned sample described in the proposal is fifteen evaluators: twelve primary STEM teachers and three subject or curriculum experts. This is a purposive feasibility sample, not a statistical sample for national generalization.
